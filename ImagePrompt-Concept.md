# ImagePrompt-Concept

Image prompts for a science-fantasy single-track vehicle whose front wheel is replaced by a vertically spinning gyro-top.

## Concept summary

The front wheel is a broad lens-shaped spinning top (point-down, vertical spin axis, an electric motor inside). It balances on a single ground-contact point that **trails behind** the raked steering axis — caster/shopping-trolley geometry — so the machine self-centres when straight and leans into the direction it travels. Rider, fuel and a combustion engine are stacked **high and aft** of the centre of gravity. The combustion engine powers the motor directly and acts as the rear counterweight; a small internal battery handles regenerative function. There is **no rear wheel** — the tail is a cantilevered boom carrying the exhaust.

---

## Shared style spine

Prepend (or keep consistent across) every prompt:

> Clean, confident unbroken ink outlines; flat planes of luminous gouache-like colour with minimal gradient; enormous quiet skies in peach, rose and turquoise; vast empty salt flats and wind-carved dunes; surreal sense of scale; the silent, sun-bleached retro-future mood of late-1970s/early-1980s French *bande dessinée* science fantasy. Matte bone-white and pale-ochre bodywork; the spinning gyro-top is the single oil-slick iridescent jewel and only glossy element in the frame.

## Vehicle design key-art

### 1. Hero in motion
Three-quarter front action shot of a lone single-track machine carving across a cracked salt flat, banked hard into its line like a sail. Its front "wheel" is a broad lens-shaped spinning top, point-down, balanced on a single luminous contact point, trailing behind a long raked steering spar. Rider crouched low; fuel tank and finned combustion engine slung high and aft over a cantilevered tail with no rear wheel. A fine plume of dust curls off the contact point. Low sun, long shadow, immense pastel sky.

### 2. Traveler at rest
A solitary figure in a loose flight-suit stands beside the parked machine on a dune ridge at dawn, dwarfed by an endless sky. The bike rests canted on a thin deployed skid, its iridescent gyro-top still slowly turning and glowing faintly, as if it never fully sleeps. Quiet, contemplative, vast negative space above.

### 3. Monumental low angle
Worm's-eye hero shot of the machine canted against a colossal eroded mesa, the tiny rider silhouetted at the controls. Emphasise the silhouette: long diving front spar to the small luminous top, heavy mechanical mass cantilevered out back, exhaust haze trailing. Strange pale moon low on the horizon.

### 4. Speed run
Side-on tracking shot, rider tucked tight, the desert highway streaking past in flat bands of colour. The gyro-top reads as a bright vertical blur of iridescence at the front; the engine aft glows warm. Motion conveyed through clean speed-lines and flattened background, not blur. Huge turquoise sky.

### 5. Dusk idle
Intimate near-twilight scene: machine stopped, gyro-top idling with a soft inner glow, the traveler crouched checking the engine. Warm key light from the gyro and engine against a deepening rose-and-indigo sky, first stars out. Stillness and faint mechanical hum implied.

### 6. Establishing wide
Extreme wide shot, the rider-and-machine a tiny mote crossing an immense empty plain under two pale suns, a single thin dust-line scribed behind the contact point stretching to the horizon. Scale and solitude are the subject.

## Technical drawings

### 7. Orthographic side elevation
Precise blueprint-style side elevation of the machine, clean thin ink linework on warm off-white, faint construction grid. Label callouts with leader lines: vertical-axis gyro-top, single ground contact, raked steering pivot axis, *trail* dimension between contact and axis ground-point, steering column, frame spar, rider position, combustion engine + fuel (marked spin-charger / counterweight), regen battery, cantilevered exhaust tail, centre-of-gravity symbol set high and aft. Restrained palette, one iridescent accent on the gyro.

### 8. Front elevation
Head-on front orthographic view showing the single-track stance: the lens-shaped top edge-on as a narrow vertical jewel meeting the ground at one point, the raked column and bars above, rider shoulders, with a faint ghosted second outline showing the machine leaning into a turn. Centreline and lean-angle dimension marked.

### 9. Three-quarter exploded view
Technical exploded axonometric: the gyro-top, steering column, frame spar, rider seat, fuel tank, engine, battery and tail boom separated along thin dashed assembly axes, each component floating in order with a small index number. Flat colour-coded by function (jewel-iridescent top, warm engine, cool battery).

### 10. Gyro-top cutaway
Detailed cross-section of the spinning top itself: outer iridescent lens shell cut away to reveal the internal electric motor on the vertical axis, the small regen battery pack, and the drive coupling where the combustion engine feeds angular momentum in. Arrows show spin direction and energy flow in (engine to motor) and out (regen to battery). Clean schematic, warm/cool flow colours.

### 11. Caster geometry detail
Zoomed engineering detail of the front end: raked steering axis as a dashed centreline projected to the ground, the top's contact point trailing behind it, *trail* and *rake angle* dimensioned, a small curved arrow showing how the contact swings to chase the travel direction like a trolley caster. Annotation note: "self-centring; leans into travel."

### 12. Drafting sheet (multi-view)
A full title-block drafting sheet combining side, front and top orthographic views plus the gyro-top detail inset, arranged on a warm parchment ground with a faint grid, border, and a hand-lettered title block naming the vehicle. Consistent thin linework throughout, single iridescent accent reserved for the gyro in each view.

*Tip: for the technical set, image models usually render one clean view more reliably than a packed multi-view sheet (#12). If a multi-view comes out muddy, generate #7, #8 and #10 separately and compose them.*
